Application of robots

Areas of Application for Robots

Industrial robots


They perform tasks to automate various production processes, improve their efficiency and quality of work (assembly, construction, foundry, packing and sorting, transport, agricultural and others).

Industrial robots include stationary or mobile automatic machines, which consist of an executive device in the form of a manipulator and a reprogrammable software control device for performing motor and control functions in the production process.[9]

Household (service) robots


They help a person in everyday life. There are two main categories among household robots:

Home robots are useful devices that make everyday life easier. Among them, personal robots such as vacuum cleaner robots, kitchen robots and pet robots stand out. They help with cleaning, cooking and provide company.[10]

Military robot


They are multifunctional technical devices that participate in combat operations. These include:

Land – sapper robots, self-propelled tanks and armored personnel carriers, full-fledged combat complexes;

Aerial – for example, robot drones (for artillery strikes, surveillance, reconnaissance, etc.);

Marine – surface and underwater vehicles that search for mines, patrol, escort, and others.[11]

Rescue robots


They are used by rapid response services. They participate in rescuing people, dismantling rubble, demining, extinguishing fires and so on.

Robots for emergency management are an important tool for rapid response to various emergency situations. They can be involved in various areas, including rescue operations, search and rescue of people from rubble, neutralization of explosive objects, as well as fire extinguishing. In situations where human access is difficult or dangerous, robots can work as an effective replacement capable of performing a number of tasks that save lives and minimize risks to humans.[12]

Medical robots

Medical robots are involved in diagnostics and surgical operations, help to manufacture medicines, care for patients, and teach people medical skills. Separately, we can talk about robotic prostheses and transplants that can replace damaged body parts, organs or tissues.[13]

Research robots

Research robots perform research in various environments such as underground spaces, water depths, space, as well as in high temperatures, radiation and other extreme environments. They conduct reconnaissance, data collection, detection of dangerous objects and perform various scientific and engineering tasks that may be inaccessible or dangerous to humans. Robots play an important role in expanding our understanding of the world around us and helping to open new horizons in scientific research and space exploration.[14]

Android robots


Android robots are robots that resemble humans both in appearance and behavior.

One of the key characteristics of androids is their appearance, which often mimics human anatomy. This includes the head, body, limbs, and sometimes even facial features such as eyes, nose, and mouth. This human-like appearance makes them more accessible to interact with people and integrate into society.

They can be used as home assistants, teaching robots in schools, personal assistants, medical assistants, or even to perform dangerous tasks that can endanger human life. [15]
